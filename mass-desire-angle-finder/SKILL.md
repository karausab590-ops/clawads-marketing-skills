# Mass Desire Angle Finder

Scale a winning product from $300-500/day to $1K+/day by finding multiple persuasive angles for the same desire. Based on Anthony's "Angle Loading" framework for dropshipping and direct response advertising.

## When to Use

You already have a product that's selling. You know the desire. Now you need MORE angles to scale — different arguments for why the customer should buy NOW, through YOUR product specifically.

**This is NOT for finding products or desires.** This is for finding angles on top of a desire you've already validated with sales.

## Core Concept: What Is an Angle?

An angle is NOT a hook. An angle is NOT a headline. An angle is:

> **Who or what you BLAME for the problem.**

Same person + same desire + different blame = different angle.

A good angle does 3 things:
1. **Reframes the problem** — introduces a new way of understanding why the avatar hasn't gotten what they want yet
2. **Implies a solution without naming the product** — builds the logical bridge so by the time the reader sees your product, it feels like the only answer
3. **Runs through the ENTIRE copy** — it's the underlying argument, not just the first line

### Quick Example (Back Pain Product)

**Desire:** I want to keep my job and provide for my family without this pain destroying me.

| Angle | What We Blame |
|-------|--------------|
| Painkillers mask the pain but make the real problem worse | Blame: painkillers / wrong treatment |
| The back pain industry is designed to keep you coming back, not solve the root problem | Blame: the industry / bigger enemy |
| There's a window where back pain is correctable and most people blow right past it | Blame: time running out / urgency |
| Doctors told you nothing is wrong, it's normal for your age | Blame: doctors / medical dismissal |
| You're stretching the wrong thing — your pain is coming from your hips, not your spine | Blame: wrong diagnosis |

Each of these is the SAME person, SAME desire, but a completely different argument for why they should take action NOW.

## How to Use

Tell me:
1. **What product you're selling** (e.g., posture corrector, hair growth serum, weight loss supplement)
2. **The winning desire** — what does your customer actually want? Not the product, but the outcome. (e.g., "I want to keep my job without back pain destroying me")
3. **Your target avatar** (optional but better) — who specifically? (e.g., construction workers aged 35-55 with chronic back pain)
4. **What's already working** (optional) — your current winning ad angle, if you have one

I will then:

### Step 1 — Deep Research
Search Reddit, forums, and medical/scientific sources for your avatar's:
- **Failed solutions** — what have they already tried that didn't work?
- **Limiting beliefs** — what do they believe about the problem that keeps them stuck?
- **Emotional triggers** — what are they afraid of? What keeps them up at night?
- **Things they blame** — who or what do THEY think is responsible?
- **Knowledge gaps** — what do they NOT know about their own problem?

I'll pull real verbatim language from real people. Not generic AI summaries.

### Step 2 — Extract Raw Angle Seeds
From the research, I'll identify every potential "blame target":
- Wrong diagnosis / wrong treatment
- A bigger enemy (industry, system, establishment)
- Time pressure / window closing
- Authority figures who failed them (doctors, trainers, gurus)
- Hidden root cause they don't know about
- Their own habits making it worse without realizing
- Something they trust that's actually sabotaging them

### Step 3 — Build 5+ Complete Angles
For each angle, I'll give you:

**Angle Name:** Short label (e.g., "The Wrong Diagnosis Angle")

**The Blame:** Who/what we're blaming (1 sentence)

**The Reframe:** How we're making the avatar see the problem differently (2-3 sentences)

**Why This Compels Action:** The logical bridge to your product — why this angle makes buying feel necessary and urgent

**Hook Examples (3):** Headlines/first lines that carry this angle

**Mini Sales Letter Outline:** A 5-point outline showing how this angle would flow through an entire ad or advertorial:
1. Open (hook carrying the angle)
2. Problem amplification (deepen the reframe)
3. Failed solutions (invalidate what they've tried)
4. Bridge to product (the logical "aha")
5. CTA (urgency tied to the angle)

**Strength Rating:** How strong this angle is (1-5) based on:
- Research backing (did real people express this?)
- Emotional weight (does it trigger fear, anger, hope?)
- Uniqueness (is anyone else running this angle?)

### Step 4 — Prioritize & Recommend
I'll rank all angles by strength and tell you which 2-3 to test first and why.

## Research Sources I'll Check
- Reddit (niche subreddits for your avatar)
- Quora threads
- Amazon reviews (for competing products)
- PubMed / medical studies (for health/beauty niches)
- Facebook groups (if accessible)
- YouTube comments on related videos
- Forum posts (niche-specific)

## Important Rules

1. **Every angle must come from REAL research.** No making up angles from thin air. Every angle needs at least one real person's words backing it up.
2. **The desire stays the same.** We're not changing what the customer wants. We're changing the ARGUMENT for why they should act now.
3. **Angles are NOT hooks.** A hook is one line. An angle is the entire persuasive argument that runs through the copy. I'll give you hooks too, but the angle is bigger than that.
4. **More specific = more powerful.** "Back pain is bad" is not an angle. "The stretches your PT gave you are making your disc compression worse" IS an angle.
5. **If the research doesn't support it, I won't force it.** Better to have 3 strong research-backed angles than 10 weak ones.
