## What This Skill Does

Gary Vaynerchuk (Gary Vee) built VaynerMedia into a $200M+ agency by understanding one thing most brands get wrong: you can't ask before you give.

His "Jab Jab Jab Right Hook" framework is the most practical social media playbook for ecommerce sellers, dropshippers, and brand builders. It tells you exactly what to post, when to post it, and when to finally ask for the sale.

This skill generates platform-native jabs AND right hooks, plus applies Gary Vee's "Document Don't Create" system so you never run out of content.

## The Core Framework

### What's a Jab vs. a Right Hook?

**JAB = Give value. No ask.**
Pure content that entertains, educates, or emotionally resonates. No pitch, no discount, no CTA to buy. Just value.

**RIGHT HOOK = Ask for the sale.**
Direct offer. Clear CTA. Specific action you want them to take. This works BECAUSE you've already given value.

The ratio Gary Vee recommends: **3-5 jabs for every 1 right hook.**

## Platform-Native Rules (Critical)

Gary Vee's biggest lesson: every platform has its own language. A right hook that works on email FAILS on TikTok. Content must be native.

### Facebook
- **Jab format:** Long-form emotional story post (200-400 words), or a shareable opinion that sparks debate. Videos: 1-3 min, autoplay-friendly, captions required
- **Right hook format:** Carousel ads with before/after, clear price + benefit in first line, "Shop Now" CTA

### Instagram
- **Jab format:** High-aesthetic single image or Reel showing behind-the-scenes, lifestyle, or quick tip. Caption = story or strong opinion
- **Right hook format:** Reel with product demo + direct "Link in bio" CTA, or Story with swipe-up/link sticker

### TikTok
- **Jab format:** Authentic, low-production-value video. Document a real moment, share an opinion, show the "how" behind your product. Hook in first 2 seconds
- **Right hook format:** Product demo showing the problem it solves, trending sound, "Get yours — link in bio" at end

### Twitter/X
- **Jab format:** Hot take, contrarian opinion, quick useful tip, or thread that teaches something counterintuitive
- **Right hook format:** Single tweet with specific offer + link. No fluff, just value prop + link

## The Jab Content Matrix

Use this to generate 5 types of jabs for any product or brand:

| Type | What It Is | Example for Posture Corrector |
|------|-----------|-------------------------------|
| **Education** | Teach something useful | "3 desk stretches that fix forward head posture in 5 min" |
| **Entertainment** | Make them laugh or feel something | "POV: You realize your shoulders are at your ears again" |
| **Inspiration** | Motivate without selling | "Back pain is optional. Most people just don't know that yet." |
| **Behind-the-scenes** | Show how things work | "We tested 7 designs before landing on this one. Here's why..." |
| **Social proof** | Customer story, no pitch | "Customer message we got this morning:" + screenshot |

## The "Document Don't Create" System

Gary Vee's content hack: **stop trying to create perfect content. Document what's already happening.**

Instead of scripting videos or planning photoshoots, document:
- Your morning routine related to your product/niche
- A customer question you answered today
- A mistake you made and what you learned
- A behind-the-scenes moment from packing, shipping, or testing

**Why it works for dropshippers:** You don't need a studio. Film your products arriving, your testing process, your reaction when a product surprises you. That IS the content.


## Real Example Output

**7-Day Plan: Wireless Ear Cleaning Camera (TikTok)**

**Day 1 (Jab - Education):**
> Caption: "The real reason Q-tips make earwax worse... (this shocked me) 🧵"
> Visual: Face-cam reaction video explaining how Q-tips push wax deeper. No product mention.

**Day 2 (Jab - Entertainment):**
> Caption: "POV: you've been cleaning your ears wrong your whole life 💀"
> Visual: Relatable reaction meme format with text overlay

**Day 3 (Jab - Behind-the-scenes):**
> Caption: "We tested 40 ear camera models. Here's what most get wrong..."
> Visual: Quick unboxing/comparison of rejected products, raw phone footage

**Day 4 (Jab - Social Proof):**
> Caption: "We didn't expect this message... 🥹"
> Visual: Screenshot of customer review/DM with their result

**Day 5 (Jab - Education):**
> Caption: "5 signs you have a buildup you can't feel"
> Visual: List-style text overlay or talking-head video

**Day 6 (Soft Jab with Product Mention):**
> Caption: "This is why I started carrying mine everywhere"
> Visual: Casual "lifestyle" clip using the product, no price, no CTA — just normalizing it

**Day 7 (Right Hook):**
> Caption: "The exact camera I used in all these videos — $29, free shipping, link in bio 🔗"
> Visual: 15-second demo showing the product working, end card with price + link

## Why This Works

Gary Vee's framework is built on one truth: **attention is the asset, not the product.**

Every jab deposits trust into your brand account. The right hook withdraws from it. If you only post right hooks (pure ads), your account goes negative — people unfollow, mute, or tune you out.

Drop 3-5 pieces of genuine value first, and when you ask, people are ready to say yes.

This is why dropshippers who run organic content alongside paid ads consistently outperform those running ads alone. The jabs warm the audience the ads close.

Apply to: TikTok + Instagram organic, Facebook page strategy, Twitter/X growth, pre-launch campaigns, email marketing, YouTube Shorts.
