## What This Skill Does

This skill generates Facebook and Instagram ad copy for dropshippers targeting cold audiences. It uses direct-response copywriting frameworks — specifically Dan Kennedy's "Magnetic Marketing" principles — adapted for ecom scroll behavior, mobile-first reading, and impulse purchase psychology.

**Who it's for:**
- Dropshippers running Facebook/Instagram paid ads
- Ecom store owners writing their own ad copy
- Media buyers who need fresh copy angles fast
- Agencies managing dropship client accounts

## Why Most Dropship Facebook Ads Fail

The typical dropship ad reads like this:

> "✅ 50% OFF TODAY ONLY! 🔥 Get our Amazing Posture Corrector NOW! FREE SHIPPING! Limited Stock! ORDER NOW!!!"

This copy is dead. Facebook users have been conditioned to scroll past it in 0.2 seconds. Here's why it fails:

1. **No specificity** — "Amazing" and "Limited Stock" are meaningless. Everyone says this.
2. **No story** — It's a billboard, not a conversation.
3. **Leads with discount** — Trains customers to wait for sales, attracts bargain hunters with high return rates.
4. **No emotional hook** — Speaks to no one's actual pain.

Dan Kennedy's core insight: **People don't buy products. They buy solutions to problems they desperately want solved.** Your ad's job is to identify the problem so precisely that the reader thinks "how did they know that about me?"

## The Kennedy-Adapted Ecom Framework

### Step 1: The Magnetic Headline
Kennedy's rules for direct response headlines, adapted for Facebook:

| Formula | Structure | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Who Else Wants** | "Who else wants [desirable result]?" | "Who else wants to sleep through the night without back pain?" |
| **The Secret Of** | "The secret of [people they admire]" | "The secret of people who never feel bloated after meals" |
| **Here Is A Method** | "Here is a [fast/easy/proven] way to [result]" | "Here's a simple way to fix your posture — without a chiropractor" |
| **Warning** | "Warning: [counterintuitive risk]" | "Warning: Your phone is slowly ruining your posture (here's the fix)" |
| **How [Blank] Made [Result]** | "How [someone like them] got [result]" | "How a WFH mom with 3 kids finally fixed her chronic neck pain" |
| **The Reason Why** | "The reason why [common thing] doesn't work — and what does" | "The reason why stretching alone won't fix your back pain" |

### Step 2: The Scroll-Stopping First Line
This is your real hook — the line that appears BEFORE "See more." It must make the reader feel:
- Understood ("That's exactly my problem")
- Curious ("Wait, what?")
- Or called out ("How do they know this about me?")

Formula: **Empathy statement + specific detail**

> "If you spend more than 4 hours a day at a desk, your posture is probably already worse than you think."

> "Most portable blenders I tried were useless for thick protein shakes. Then I found this one."

> "Nobody tells you that LED lights from big box stores use cheap drivers that flicker — and that flickering is why you get headaches."

### Step 3: Problem Agitation
After the hook, make the problem feel bigger. Not fake-bigger — *true-bigger*. Use specifics.

- Don't: "Back pain is really bad"
- Do: "By 35, most office workers have measurably reduced disc spacing. Sitting 8 hours a day compresses your spine the same way carrying a 20-pound backpack does — except you do it every single day."

### Step 4: The Solution Bridge
Transition from problem to product WITHOUT making it feel like a commercial.

Bridge phrases:
- "That's why I started looking for something different."
- "That's the reason [X] people have switched to..."
- "The breakthrough was finding a [category] that actually works because [mechanism]."

### Step 5: Social Proof Integration
Cold traffic doesn't trust you. Borrow trust from:
- Review counts ("4,200+ verified reviews")
- Specific review quotes ("One customer wrote: 'I wore this for 3 days and my coworker asked if I'd been to the chiropractor'")
- Authority ("Recommended by physical therapists")
- Momentum ("23,000 units sold this month")

### Step 6: Scarcity / Urgency (Use Honestly)
Real scarcity: "We ordered 500 units for this promotion. 340 sold in 48 hours."
Real urgency: "This price expires Sunday — we go back to full price then."

Don't fake it. Today's buyers can smell fake scarcity.

### Step 7: The CTA
One action. Clear. Low friction.
- "Click below to check availability"
- "See if it's still in stock →"
- "Try it risk-free for 30 days →"

### FORMAT 3: Long Ad (Conversion — 250-350 words)
Best for: Warm retargeting, Facebook right column, people who visited your store, email audiences

```
[MAGNETIC HEADLINE]

[STORY OPEN — relatability, name a real scenario]

[PROBLEM DEEP DIVE — give specifics, make it feel true]

[FALSE SOLUTIONS — name 2 things they've probably tried that failed]
```

## Ad Copy Don'ts (Specific to Dropshipping)

- ❌ Don't mention AliExpress, CJ, or your supplier — ever
- ❌ Don't use "dropshipping" language in the ad
- ❌ Don't say "our store" — say "we" or the brand name
- ❌ Don't stack emoji (1-2 max per ad, none in the headline)
- ❌ Don't use ALL CAPS except for the most critical word
- ❌ Don't make claims you can't back up ("clinically proven" without citations)
