## The Core Negotiation Framework

### The 3P Principle: Position, Proof, Path Forward

Every supplier negotiation email must contain all three:

**Position** — What you want and why you deserve it (volume potential, long-term relationship, existing sales data)

**Proof** — Evidence you're serious (sales screenshots, store stats, current order history, competitor quotes)

**Path Forward** — A clear ask with a specific number, a deadline, and an easy yes (don't make them guess what you want)

### The Supplier Mindset

Chinese suppliers think in volume and certainty. They will give you better prices if:
- You can show current sales velocity (even 5-10 orders/day gets attention)
- You signal you're building long-term, not testing
- You give them a specific volume commitment (even estimated)
- You make the negotiation feel easy and professional

They WON'T respond well to:
- Vague "I might order a lot"
- Demanding tone without leverage
- Asking for too many concessions at once

## Script Templates

### Script 1: Cold Outreach to AliExpress/1688 Supplier

**Use when:** You found a product and want to contact the manufacturer directly to get wholesale pricing.

```
Subject: Wholesale Inquiry - [Product Name] - USA/[Country] Store

Hi [Supplier Name / Team],

My name is [Your Name] and I run an e-commerce store in [Country] selling [product category]. I've been researching suppliers for [specific product] and your product [Product Link or Reference ID] looks like a strong match.

I'm currently selling [X units/week OR "planning to launch with an initial order of X units"]. If quality and pricing work, I expect to scale to [X units/month] within 60 days.

I'd like to discuss:
- Your best wholesale price for [X] units/month
- Whether you offer white-label / custom packaging
- Your standard processing time and shipping options to [Country]

Can you send me your price list and MOQ? I'm ready to move quickly once we agree on terms.

Best,
[Your Name]
[Store Name / Website]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Using the dropship supplier cold outreach template, write me an email for a supplier selling [product name] on AliExpress. My store is in [country], I currently sell [X orders/week or I'm launching], and I want to ask about pricing, white label, and shipping time. Keep it professional but not corporate."

### Script 2: Price Negotiation (You Already Have a Supplier)

**Use when:** You're already ordering from this supplier but want a lower unit price, especially after proving sales volume.

```
Subject: Price Review Request - Order History Attached

Hi [Supplier Name],

I've been ordering [product name] from you for [X months]. Over this time, I've placed [X orders totaling $X or X units]. Thank you for the consistent quality and service.

I'm now scaling my marketing and expect volume to increase significantly over the next 30-60 days. I'd like to discuss a revised pricing structure to support this growth.

My current unit price is $[X]. Based on [our order history / competitor quotes / industry standard], I'd like to request a price of $[Y] per unit for orders of [Z+ units/month].

In return, I'm happy to:
- Commit to a minimum monthly volume of [X units]
- Pay [upfront / on a set schedule] to reduce your risk
- Give you first right of refusal on new products I source

Can we hop on a quick call or exchange a few messages to lock this in?

Looking forward to your reply.

[Your Name]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Write a supplier price negotiation email. I've been buying [product] from [supplier] for [X months], I order about [X units/month], and my current price is $[X]. I want to get it down to $[Y]. I'm willing to commit to [volume] per month. Use the 3P framework - show proof of my history, make a specific ask, and make it easy to say yes."

### Script 3: MOQ Reduction Request

**Use when:** A supplier has a minimum order quantity (e.g., 100 units) that's too high for your testing phase.

```
Subject: MOQ Discussion - Test Order Request

Hi [Supplier],

I came across your [product name] and I'm very interested in testing it for my store. Your quality looks strong and I've seen good reviews on similar products.

I understand your MOQ is [X units]. For a first order, I'd like to request a test batch of [Y units] to validate the product with my customer base.

Here's my situation: I move fast when something works. My last winning product went from 10 test units to 200+/month within 6 weeks. I'm not looking for a one-time deal — I want a reliable long-term supplier.

If you can accept [Y units] for this first order at [price point], I'll prioritize you for scale orders once I validate demand.

Is this something you can work with?

[Your Name]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Write an MOQ negotiation email to a supplier. Their MOQ is [X units] but I only want to test with [Y units]. I want to show that I'm serious and can scale fast. Use the dropship MOQ script template. Product: [product name]. Country: [yours]."

### Script 4: Exclusive Deal / No-Compete Request

**Use when:** Your product is winning and you want to lock out competitors by getting an exclusive supply agreement.

```
Subject: Exclusive Partnership Proposal - [Product Name]

Hi [Supplier],

We've been working together for [X months] now, and as you've seen, our volume has grown from [X] to [Y] units/month.

I'd like to have a conversation about an exclusive arrangement. Here's what I'm proposing:

We commit to a minimum of [X units/month] for the next [6/12] months. In exchange, you agree not to fulfill orders for [this specific product / this product with our custom spec] to any other stores in [Country/Region] during that period.

This protects your volume commitment from us, and protects our business investment in marketing and scaling this product.

I'm happy to formalize this with a simple written agreement if that helps.

Can we discuss terms?

[Your Name]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Write an exclusive supplier deal email. I've been ordering [product] for [X months], volume is now [Y units/month]. I want to propose exclusivity in [country] for 6 months in exchange for a volume commitment of [X units/month]. Use the exclusive deal script template."

### Script 5: Sample Request (New Supplier)

**Use when:** You want to order product samples before committing to bulk orders.

```
Subject: Sample Order Request - [Product Name]

Hi,

I'm evaluating [product name] for my e-commerce store in [Country]. Before placing a larger order, I'd like to receive [1-3] samples to check quality.

Please confirm:
- Sample price per unit (or if samples are free)
- Shipping cost to [your address or just your country]
- Estimated delivery time
- Whether sample cost is deductible from first bulk order

I'm evaluating 2-3 suppliers right now and plan to decide within [1-2 weeks]. Quick response appreciated.

[Your Name]
[Store/Email]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Write a sample request email to an AliExpress or Alibaba supplier for [product name]. I want [X] samples shipped to [country]. Ask about sample cost, shipping, timeline, and whether the cost is credited toward bulk orders. Keep it short and professional."

### Script 6: Quality Complaint + Compensation Request

**Use when:** You received a shipment with quality issues (wrong items, damaged goods, poor quality vs. listing).

```
Subject: Quality Issue - Order #[Order Number] - Compensation Required

Hi [Supplier],

I received order #[Order Number] on [Date]. Unfortunately, [X% / X out of Y units] have quality issues. Specifically: [describe the issue clearly — e.g., "zipper defective on 8 of 20 units", "color differs significantly from listing photos", "3 units arrived damaged"].

I've attached photos for your reference.

This has resulted in [customer refunds / negative reviews / lost sales]. I need this resolved to continue our business relationship.

I'm requesting one of the following:
Option A: Replacement of [X] defective units at no cost
Option B: Refund of $[X] (proportional to the defective rate)
Option C: Credit toward my next order of equal value

Please let me know which option you can accommodate. I'd like to resolve this quickly and keep our relationship moving forward.

[Your Name]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Write a supplier quality complaint email. Order number: [X]. I received [product] and [describe the issue]. I want either replacements, a refund, or a credit. Use a firm but professional tone. Include three options for resolution so they have an easy choice."

### Script 7: Reorder with Better Terms

**Use when:** You're placing a significant reorder and want to negotiate faster processing, better pricing, or updated payment terms.

```
Subject: Reorder Ready - Let's Discuss Terms

Hi [Supplier],

Ready to place my next order for [product name] — looking at approximately [X units].

Before I confirm, I'd like to quickly revisit a few things:

1. **Pricing:** Can we do $[X] per unit given this volume? (Our last price was $[Y])
2. **Processing time:** My last order took [X days] to ship. Can we get it down to [Y days] this time?
3. **Payment:** Currently paying [method]. Would you accept [preferred method] for this order?

I can send payment today if we align on terms. Let me know.

[Your Name]
```

**AI Prompt to customize:**
> "Write a reorder negotiation email. I'm ready to order [X units] of [product]. I want to negotiate the price down from $[current] to $[target], get processing time under [X days], and possibly change payment method to [method]. Keep it short, businesslike, use the reorder terms script."

## Full Workflow: From Cold Supplier to Exclusive Partner

```
Stage 1: Discovery → Use Script 5 (Sample Request) or Script 1 (Cold Outreach)
Stage 2: First Order → No negotiation needed, just build the relationship
Stage 3: Proven Sales (10+ orders/day) → Use Script 2 (Price Negotiation)
Stage 4: Scale (50+ orders/day) → Use Script 3 (MOQ Reduction) if still relevant
Stage 5: Winning Product → Use Script 4 (Exclusive Deal)
Stage 6: Any Quality Issue → Use Script 6 (Quality Complaint)
Stage 7: Every Reorder → Use Script 7 (Reorder Terms)
```

## Power Tactics Every Dropshipper Should Know

**The Competitor Quote Tactic**
Mention that you've received quotes from other suppliers. You don't have to be lying — go get 2-3 quotes and use them. "I have quotes from two other suppliers at $X. I prefer working with you, but I need you to match this."

**The Volume Escalation Promise**
Always frame current volume as the floor, not the ceiling. "I'm at 30 orders/day now. I expect to scale to 100+/day within 60 days with our upcoming campaign." Suppliers give better prices when they see upside.

**The Long-Term Framing**
Emphasize you want a "long-term partner," not a one-off vendor. This shifts the dynamic from transactional to relational. Suppliers price differently for repeat customers.

**The Easy Yes Close**
Don't make them think. Give them a specific number, a specific commitment, and ask a yes/no question. "Can you do $8.50/unit for 500 units/month?" is 100x better than "What's your best price?"

**Payment Upfront Leverage**
Offering to pay upfront (instead of on delivery) is a huge negotiating chip with smaller suppliers. They get cash flow certainty. Use this to extract 5-15% price reductions.
