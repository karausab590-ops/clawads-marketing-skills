## The Brunson Funnel Frameworks

Understanding WHY these frameworks work is what separates copy that converts from copy that looks good but doesn't sell.

### Framework 1: Hook-Story-Offer (The Master Structure)

Every single piece of Brunson's funnel copy follows this three-part structure — whether it's a 2-second ad or a 2-hour webinar.

**HOOK** — Gets attention. Makes the right person stop.
- Must speak to a desire, fear, or curiosity
- Calls out the specific avatar ("If you're a coach who...")
- Creates an open loop the brain needs to close

**STORY** — Creates belief change. The purpose of the story is not entertainment — it's to destroy the #1 false belief that is stopping your prospect from buying.
- Your story is proof that the thing you're selling works
- The story must be relatable (same starting position as your prospect)
- The transformation in the story = the transformation you're selling

**OFFER** — Closes the sale. After story has built desire and belief, the offer should feel obvious.
- Stack the value so price becomes irrelevant
- Always anchor against the alternative cost
- Make the CTA clear and singular

### Framework 2: The Epiphany Bridge

This is Brunson's core story framework. The goal: make the prospect experience YOUR "aha moment" as if it were their own.

**The 8-Step Epiphany Bridge Story:**
1. **The backstory** — Where were you before the discovery? (Must match where prospect is now)
2. **The desire** — What did you want? (External goal — the thing your prospect wants)
3. **The wall** — What was the obstacle? (The same obstacle your prospect faces)
4. **The epiphany** — What was the "aha moment"? (The insight that changed everything)
5. **The plan** — What did you do differently after the epiphany?
6. **The conflict** — What challenges did you face even after the epiphany?
7. **The achievement** — What result did you get?
8. **The transformation** — Who are you NOW vs. who you were?

**Why it works:** People don't buy based on logic — they buy based on emotion, then justify with logic. The Epiphany Bridge makes them FEEL the transformation before they pay for it. When they feel the epiphany themselves, they believe it will work for them.

### Framework 3: The Big Domino

Before writing any funnel copy, identify the ONE core belief that must be true for the prospect to buy. Brunson calls this "the big domino."

**The Big Domino Statement:**
"If I can make [target audience] believe that [my new opportunity] is the key to [their desired result] AND is only achievable through [my specific mechanism], then all other objections become irrelevant."

**Examples:**
- "If I can make gym owners believe that lead generation funnels — not referrals — are the key to predictable revenue growth, then pricing, time, and trust objections disappear."
- "If I can make coaches believe that selling $10K packages is easier than selling $1K packages, then objections about their market and their worth disappear."

**Why it works:** Every sales page, VSL, and webinar is really a campaign to knock down ONE domino. When that one belief falls, everything else (objections, pricing resistance, skepticism) falls with it. Most copywriters try to overcome every objection. Brunson identifies the ONE domino that makes every other objection irrelevant.

### Framework 4: The Stack (Offer Presentation)

The Stack is how Brunson presents an offer so the value is obviously enormous and the price is obviously small.

**The Stack Structure:**
1. **Element 1** — The core product/service. Introduce it. Give it a standalone value.
2. **"But wait, that's not all"** — Add element 2. Give it its own value. Running total.
3. Continue stacking elements, each with individual value.
4. **"You're getting all of this for just..."** — The real price, against the stacked total.
5. **Urgency/Scarcity** — Why this price only exists right now.
6. **Guarantee** — Remove all risk.

**Why it works:** People make purchase decisions by comparing value to price. The Stack makes that comparison overwhelmingly in favor of buying by anchoring the "true value" high before revealing the actual price. The price feels like a no-brainer because the contrast is so large.

### Framework 5: The Perfect Webinar (3 Secrets Structure)

Brunson's webinar script structure. Works for VSLs, Zoom calls, and live presentations.

**The Opening (10-15 mins):**
- Hook: Bold promise about what they'll learn
- Introduce yourself via Epiphany Bridge story
- Tease the 3 secrets

**Secret 1: The Opportunity (Vehicle)**
- Destroy the false belief about the METHOD/VEHICLE
- Example: "Email is dead" → destroy this belief
- Introduce your method as the NEW opportunity

**Secret 2: The Internal Belief (You Can Do It)**
- Destroy the false belief about THEMSELVES
- "I'm not smart/experienced/tech-savvy enough"
- Epiphany bridge story showing someone just like them succeeded

**Secret 3: The External Belief (The Market)**
- Destroy the false belief about EXTERNAL FACTORS
- "The market is too competitive / The timing is wrong"
- Proof that now is the best time, this market is the best opportunity

**The Close:**
- The Stack presentation
- Price anchor + price reveal
- Scarcity/urgency
- Guarantee
- Final CTA
