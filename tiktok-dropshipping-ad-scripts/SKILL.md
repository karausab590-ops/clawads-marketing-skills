# tiktok-dropshipping-ad-scripts

> Write scroll-stopping TikTok and Facebook video ad scripts for dropshipping products -- using the exact proven formulas that 7-figure dropshippers use to scale fast.

## What This Skill Does

This skill turns any dropshipping product into a ready-to-film video ad script. It applies the 5 core short-form ad formulas used by top dropshippers on TikTok and Facebook Reels. No fluff -- every output is a word-for-word script your UGC creator can read straight to camera.

## The 5 Core Formulas

### Formula 1: Problem-Agitate-Reveal (PAR)

Best for: pain-point products (posture, sleep, pain relief, skincare)

Structure: Hook (0-3s) -> Agitate (3-8s) -> Reveal product (8-15s) -> Proof (15-25s) -> CTA (25-30s)

Template:
Hook: "If you [SPECIFIC PAINFUL SITUATION], you need to watch this."
Agitate: "I used to [DESCRIBE HOW BAD IT WAS]. I tried [failed solution]. Nothing worked."
Reveal: "Then I found [PRODUCT]. It [simple mechanism]."
Proof: "[Result] in just [timeframe]. My [friend/coworker] couldn't believe it."
CTA: "Link in bio. Almost sold out -- grab yours before they're gone."

Example (posture corrector):
Hook: "If you sit at a desk all day and your neck is always killing you, watch this."
Agitate: "I spent $400 on massages. Tried every pillow on Amazon. Still woke up in pain."
Reveal: "Then I tried this posture corrector. It gently pulls your shoulders back -- you feel the difference in 10 minutes."
Proof: "After 2 weeks my neck pain was basically gone. My chiropractor asked what I changed."
CTA: "It's in my bio -- less than 30 bucks, running low on stock."

### Formula 2: Before/After Transformation

Best for: beauty, fitness, home organization, pet products

Template:
Hook: "My [thing] used to [before state] -- until I found this."
Transition: "I was skeptical. But after [timeframe]..."
After: "Now [specific transformed result]. I [emotional payoff]."
Mechanism: "It works by [simple 1-sentence explanation]."
CTA: "Link's in my bio. [Limited stock urgency]."

Example (kitchen organizer):
Hook: "My kitchen counter was an embarrassing disaster -- until I found this."
Transition: "I was skeptical it would fit everything, but after one afternoon..."
After: "Now every single thing has a spot. I can actually cook without moving 6 things to find my spatula."
Mechanism: "It stacks and locks so nothing shifts, even in a small kitchen."
CTA: "Link in bio. Ordered the 3-pack -- it sold out last time so grab it now."

### Formula 3: Skeptic-to-Believer

Best for: products with strong reviews, anything skeptics doubt

Template:
Hook: "I never do this but I have to be honest about [PRODUCT]."
Skepticism: "When I saw it on my feed I thought [skeptical thought]. Spent [price] thinking it would end up in a drawer."
Tried it: "First time I used it, [immediate first impression]."
Result: "[Specific result]. [Reaction from others]."
CTA: "It's in my bio. Not sponsored -- just couldn't not share this."

Example (LED face mask):
Hook: "I never do this but I have to be honest about this LED mask."
Skepticism: "When I saw it I thought it looked ridiculous. Bought it anyway because my skin was breaking out constantly."
Tried it: "First session felt weird but my skin felt tighter the next morning."
Result: "Three weeks in, my hormonal acne went from constant to almost zero. My coworker asked if I got a facial."
CTA: "Link in my bio. Not sponsored -- just the first product in 2 years that actually worked for me."

### Formula 4: Comparison/Diss Ad

Best for: products with obvious overpriced competitors

Template:
Hook: "Stop paying [high price] for [expensive brand]."
Problem: "Most people don't know [the thing they're buying] is just [commodity] with a logo on it."
Reveal: "This is [YOUR PRODUCT]. Does the exact same thing. [Price comparison]."
Comparison: "[Feature 1] -- same. [Feature 2] -- same. [Feature 3] -- actually better."
CTA: "Link in bio. [Urgency]."

Example:
Hook: "Stop paying $40 for a BlenderBottle."
Problem: "It's plastic with a wire ball in it. That's it. You're paying for the brand."
Reveal: "This is the same concept for $8. Same BPA-free plastic, same mixing ball, same leak-proof lid."
Comparison: "Holds the same amount -- same. Dishwasher safe -- same. Doesn't leak -- actually better."
CTA: "Link in my bio. Ordered 3 as backup. Basically giving these away."

### Formula 5: POV Lifestyle Hook

Best for: aspirational products (fashion, travel, home decor)

Template:
Hook: "POV: you finally [desired outcome] with [PRODUCT]."
Lifestyle: "It's [time/situation]. You're [doing aspirational thing]. [Sensory detail]."
Features: "[Feature 1] so [benefit]. [Feature 2] so [benefit]."
Emotional: "This is what [feeling] feels like."
CTA: "Link in bio -- [short urgency note]."

Example (travel cubes):
Hook: "POV: you never check a bag again."
Lifestyle: "It's 6am at the airport. You walk straight to security. One carry-on. Everything where you packed it."
Features: "Compression cubes so you fit a week into a personal item. Color-coded so you never dig for anything."
Emotional: "This is what traveling without stress feels like."
CTA: "Link in my bio. The set usually sells out."

## Abandoned Cart Recovery Ad

For retargeting people who viewed but didn't buy:

Template:
Hook: "Still thinking about [PRODUCT]? Here's what you need to know."
Objection kill: "I know what you're thinking -- [objection]. Here's the thing: [reframe]."
Social proof: "[X] people bought this in the last [timeframe]. Reviews say [specific thing]."
Urgency: "[Real reason to act now -- sale, stock, price increase]."
CTA: "Link in bio. [Discount code]. Don't let someone else grab your [size/color]."

Example (phone case):
Hook: "Still thinking about this case? Here's what you need to know."
Objection kill: "I know $35 feels like a lot for a phone case. But I've dropped my phone 4 times this month -- zero cracks."
Social proof: "3,200 reviews, 4.8 stars. Most say: wish I'd bought it sooner."
Urgency: "They're running a 20% off sale right now. No idea when it ends."
CTA: "Link in bio. Use code SAVE20."
